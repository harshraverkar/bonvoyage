Konark Sun Temple - Odisha

The Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century CE Sun Temple at Konark in Odisha, India. It is believed to have been built by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The temple is dedicated to the Hindu sun god Surya.

The temple complex is designed in the shape of a gigantic chariot with elaborately carved stone wheels, pillars, and walls. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the finest examples of Kalinga architecture.

Visitors to the Konark Sun Temple can marvel at its intricate stone carvings depicting various mythological scenes, celestial beings, and daily life activities. The temple also hosts a yearly dance festival, the Konark Dance Festival, showcasing classical Indian dance forms.
